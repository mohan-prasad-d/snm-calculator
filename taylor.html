Derivatives & Parameters
STEP 1 — Select Taylor expansion order ?
Taylor Order
Determines how many derivative terms are included in the expansion.

1st: yₙ₊₁ = yₙ + h·y'
2nd: adds (h²/2!)·y''
3rd: adds (h³/3!)·y'''
4th: adds (h⁴/4!)·y''''

Higher order = more accurate but needs more derivatives.
Higher order = higher accuracy. 3rd or 4th order recommended.
STEP 2 — Enter derivative expressions in terms of x and y ?
Derivative Expressions
Enter each derivative as a math expression using x and y.

Example:
y' = x+y → enter: x + y
y'' = 1+x+y → enter: 1 + x + y

Supports: x, y, +, -, *, /, ^,
sin, cos, tan, exp, log, sqrt, abs
y′ = dy/dx  (1st derivative — required)
Power: x^2   Trig: sin(x) cos(x)   Other: exp(x) log(x) sqrt(x)
y″ = d²y/dx²  (2nd derivative)
Compute y'' analytically by differentiating y' with respect to x.
y‴ = d³y/dx³  (3rd derivative)
Compute y''' by differentiating y'' with respect to x.
y‰ = d⁴y/dx⁴  (4th derivative)
Compute y'''' by differentiating y''' with respect to x.

Method Overview
TAYLOR'S METHOD — CORE FORMULA
yₙ₊₁ = yₙ + h · y′
      + (h²/2!) · y″
      + (h³/3!) · y‴
      + (h⁴/4!) · y‰

where  h = step size  ·  y′,y″,y‴,y‰ = derivatives at (xₙ,yₙ)
TERM COEFFICIENTS (h/n!)
①  Term 1:  h/1! = h · y′
②  Term 2:  h²/2  · y″
③  Term 3:  h³/6  · y‴
④  Term 4:  h⁴/24 · y‰
⑤  Sum:  yₙ₊₁ = yₙ + ∑(Terms)
Properties of Taylor's Method:
▸ Order-p accurate: error ∝ h^p per step
▸ 4th-order: global error is O(h⁴)
▸ Requires explicit higher derivatives
▸ More accurate than Euler for same h
▸ Generalizes Taylor series expansion
KEY INSIGHT
Taylor's method extends Euler by including curvature and higher-order bend information, producing a much more accurate numerical trajectory.
More derivative terms → exponentially smaller error for same step size h
